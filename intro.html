What is Breaking Bad?

Breaking Bad is an American drama television show produced by Vince Gilligan. The show first aired in 2008 and ended in 2013 after 5 seasons, including a total of 62 episodes. 6 years after the last episode of the show in 2019 a Breaking Bad movie was released, called El Camino. The show has also spawned a spin-off television show, Better Call Saul, which follows the life of on of Breaking Bad's most beloved characters, Saul Goodman, set before the events of Breaking Bad.

The Story

The story of Breaking Bad sounds rather simple on paper, yet it stands at the basis of one of televisions greatest shows ever made. Walter White, age 50, lives a simple life with his wife, Skyler White and son, Walter Jr. He works as a chemistry teacher in high school. This is until he is suddenly diagnosed with terminal lung cancer. His inevitable death makes him realise he won't be leaving much behind in terms of financial support for his family.

Walt's brother-in-law, Hank, works at the Drug Enforcement Administration. When Walt comes on a ride-along with Hank to see him "bring down a meth lab" Walt realises there is a lot of money to be made selling drugs. He also finds out on of his former students, Jesse Pinkman, works as a drug manufacturer and dealer. Walt find Jesse and tells him about his plan. Jesse thinks Walt must have lost his mind and does not think it is a good plan. However, Walt convinces Jesse to give him a try anyways and so they become partners in crime.

Chemistry

Walter White describes chemistry as "the study of change" and this perfectlly encapsulates the story of Breaking Bad. It shows us how certain situations and conditions can affect a person and how much people can change when they're desperate. As you watch the show you will see Walter go through a transformation of his own. Changing from a simple family man to something he would have never even expected himself to become.
